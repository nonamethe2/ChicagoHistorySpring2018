Extravagant brownstones and many other styles lined the streets. This was the place for the wealthy to show off their riches in a secluded area from the downtown business district. Exclusive families such as the Palmers, McCormicks, and Ryersons occupied the coast along with many influential people to the city and Gold coast's development until the Michigan bridge was built, granting more access to the coast in the year of 1920.
